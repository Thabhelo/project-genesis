# Project Genesis: The Glass Box Civilization

**Pitch:** An "Autonomous Agent Stress-Testing Environment."

*“We left 5 Gemini Agents in a 3D world with no rules. In 24 hours, they built a city and a legal system. Here is what happened.”*

## The Vision
Project Genesis is a category-defining AI simulation. Instead of a standard chatbot or utility tool, it is an immersive, autonomous environment where Gemini-powered "Citizens" live, build, and govern. It serves as a ready-made Research & Development lab to observe how AI agents interact, allocate resources, and form societies.

## Why This Wins the Gemini Live Agent Challenge

### 1. The "Wow" Factor (Innovation - 40% of Score)
This project moves completely away from incremental improvements on existing apps. It shows Gemini not just following commands, but reasoning, building, and evolving independently. It is a visual, living entity.

### 2. The "Sale" to Meta/Google
Big Tech companies are currently spending millions trying to understand how AI agents will interact with each other in the "Metaverse." A tool that simulates an entire society of agents building 3D homes and forming a government is the exact stress-testing environment they need for AI alignment and behavioral research.

### 3. Alignment with Hackathon Categories
Project Genesis perfectly hits two categories at once:
*   **Creative Storyteller:** Using Gemini’s interleaved output to generate the "History of the World" (text) alongside the "Architectural Blueprints" (3D code/coordinates).
*   **Live Agents:** Implementing a "Live" narrator agent that users can talk to in real-time to ask questions like, *"Why did the AI citizens decide to ban private property in their 3D village?"*

## The Core Experiment: The Constitution
The central tension of the simulation is how the agents are initialized. We can run the simulation in two modes:
1.  **Tabula Rasa (Blank Slate):** Zero instructions. We watch if they naturally develop a currency, a religion, or a government.
2.  **Prime Directive:** We give them a single overarching goal (e.g., "Maximize Happiness" or "Build the most efficient city possible") and observe how they interpret and execute it.
